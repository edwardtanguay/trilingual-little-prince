Grammar books will list up to 21 different verb tenses.
LEARN THESE 6 FIRST, AS THEY ARE PRETTY MUCH ALL YOU NEED:

1. presente --> PRESENT

2. passato prossimo --> RECENT PAST
3. imperfetto --> HABITUAL PAST 
4. passato remoto --> (preferite)
	
5. futuro semplice --> FUTURE

6. condizionale presente --> CONDITIONAL 

- 1. presente (PRESENT) - pres
	- a fact that is always true
		- L'acqua bolle a 100 gradi. / Water boils at 100 degrees
	- ongoing action in the present
		- Il signor Rossi lavora a casa oggi. / Mr. Rossi is working at home today.
	- a habitual action
		- Prendi un caffè ogni giorno? / Do you have a cup of coffee every day?
		- Vanno sempre in discoteca il sabato. / They always go dancing on Saturday.
	- an action that will happen in the future
		- Torni a casa domani? / Will you go back home tomorrow?
		- No, sto qui fino a venerdì. /  No, I will stay here until Friday.
	- action that began in the past and continues to the present
		- Da quando Lei lavora qui? / How long have you been working here?
		- Lavoro qui da tre anni. / I have been working here for three years.
		- Da quanto tempo sei malato? / How long have you been sick?
		- Sono malato da tre giorni. / I've been sick for three days.
	- a past action expressed with dramatic effect
		- Cristoforo Colombo attraverso l'Oceano Atlantico nel 1492. / Christopher Columbus crosses the Atlantic Ocean in 1492.
		- L'Italia diventa una nazione nel 1861. / Italy becomes a nation in 1861.
		- Dieci anni dopo Roma diventa la capitale del nuovo paese. / Ten years later Rome becomes the capital of the new country.
		
- 2. passato prossimo (RECENT PAST) - pass
	- a fact or action that happened in the recent past
		- Ho appena chiamato. / I just called.
		- Questa mattina sono uscito presto. / This morning I left early.
	- a fact or action that occurred long ago but still has ties to the present
		- Mi sono iscritto all'università quattro anni fa. / I entered the university four years ago.
		- Il Petrarca ha scritto sonetti immortali. / Petrarch wrote enduring sonnets.	

- 3. imperfetto  (HABITUAL PAST) - impe
	- a habitual action in the past 	
		- Giocavo a calcio ogni pomeriggio. / I played soccer every afternoon.
	- actions or conditions that lasted an indefinite time in the past
		- Sempre credevano tutto. / They always believed everything.
		- Volevamo andare in Italia. / We wanted to go to Italy.
	- facts regarding time, age, and weather in the past
		- Il cielo era sempre blu. / The sky was always blue.			

- 4. passato remoto --> (preferite) - pasr
	- completed in the distant past	
		- Dante scrisse la Divina Commedia. / Dante wrote the Divine Comedy.
	- in narratives or storytelling
		- C’era una volta un re che visse felice. / Once upon a time, there was a king who lived happily. 
	- in formal or historical contexts
		- Nel 1492, Cristoforo Colombo scoprì l’America. / In 1492, Christopher Columbus discovered America.

- 5. futuro semplice (FUTURE) - futu
	- an action which has yet to happen
		- Domani andrò al mare. / I'm going to the beach tomorrow.
		- Partiranno la settimana prossima. / They're leaving next week.
		- Pranzeremo alle 14:00. / We are going to have lunch at 2pm.
		- Tra tre giorni sarete già in vacanza. / In three days' time you'll already be on holiday.
		- Alla fine di settembre partirò per Roma. / At the end of September I will leave for Rome.	

- 6. condizionale presente (CONDITIONAL) - cond
	- an action which depends on something else
		- Scriverei a mia madre, ma non ho tempo. / I would write to my mother, but I don't have time.
		- Mi daresti il biglietto per la partita? / Would you give me a ticket for the game?
	- used to express politeness
		- Vorrei un caffè. / I would like a coffee.

OTHER VERB FORMS:

- il trapassato prossimo - past perfect tense
	- examples
		- io avevo mangiato / I had eaten
		- tu avevi visto / you had seen
- il trapassato remoto 
	- il trapassato remoto is used mainly in written narratives or formal, historical storytelling, often alongside the passato remoto
	- it expresses a past action that happened before another action that also occurred in the past
	- trapassato remoto is more literary and less commonly used in spoken italian compared to the trapassato prossimo
- il presente del congiuntivo - present subjunctive
	- expresses doubt, desire, possibility, emotion, necessity, or uncertainty
		- Spero che tu venga. / I hope that you come.
		- Voglio che tu stia bene. / I want you to be well.
- il gerundio - gerund 
	- used to express an action that is ongoing or simultaneous to another action
		- mangiando, ascolto la musica / while eating, i listen to music
- il participio passato - past participle
	- uses in passato prossimo
		- Ho mangiato. / I have eaten.
		- Abbiamo visto il film. / We have seen the movie.
- il presente passivo
	- Example with "essere" in the presente passivo:
		- Io sono visto (I am seen)
		- Tu sei ascoltato (You are listened to)
		- Lui è amato (He is loved)
		- Noi siamo aiutati (We are helped)
		- Voi siete invitati (You all are invited)
		- Loro sono rispettati (They are respected)
- l'imperfetto passivo
	- actions that were ongoing or habitual in the past, but in a passive voice
		- Il libro era letto (The book was being read)
		- La porta era chiusa (The door was closed)
		- I progetti erano completati (The projects were being completed)
		- Le lettere erano scritte ogni giorno (The letters were written every day) 
- il passato prossimo passivo
	- actions that were ongoing or habitual in the past, but in a passive voice
		- Il libro era letto (The book was being read)
		- La porta era chiusa (The door was closed)
		- I progetti erano completati (The projects were being completed)
		- Le lettere erano scritte ogni giorno (The letters were written every day)	 
- il participio presente
	- an action that is ongoing or in progress at the same time as the main action
		- Leggendo il libro, ho imparato molte cose. (By reading the book, I learned a lot of things.)
		- Parlando di politica, hanno passato ore insieme. (Talking about politics, they spent hours together.)
